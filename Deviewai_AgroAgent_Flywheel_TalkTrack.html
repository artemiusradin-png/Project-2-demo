The Flywheel — what to say on the call Strategy: open concrete, hold the loop back. Deploy the flywheel only if they ask "why does this compound / why you?"

How to use this. Lead with the Open — concrete, time-bound, no flywheel language. Walk Beats 1–2 as the default story. Beat 3 (the compounding loop) is gated — only open it if the buyer asks about defensibility or value over time. When you do, the honest caveat goes with it, every time.

1The Open — always lead here

Say
verbatim
“Your consultant takes a photo, the system finds the right product in your 20,000-item catalog and builds the quote — what used to take 40 minutes happens before the farmer walks back to their truck.”

Concrete. Time-bound. No "AI", no "flywheel", no "platform". This sentence alone justifies the call. Then — and only then — walk the beats.

2The Arc — Beats 1 & 2 are the default story

Beat 1 · The moment of truth
A consultant outside Poltava. A farmer shows him yellowing leaves. Right now he's guessing — or calling someone who's also guessing. He photographs it; Agro-Vision reads it: the problem, the treatment, the product. The farmer sees expertise, not a salesperson. Trust is earned in thirty seconds.
Beat 2 · Trust becomes revenue
That recommendation doesn't go in a notebook. Agro-Flow writes the quote into Salesforce while the consultant is still in the field. No back-office, no lag. The sale moves at the moment the farmer believes.
▼ Beat 3 — GATED · open only if they ask "why does this compound?" / "why can't a competitor copy this?"
Beat 3 · The loop tightens. Every diagnosis and every sale feeds Agro-Insight. Your suppliers see what's actually failing in the fields this season — they stock better, price better, partner deeper. Which makes next month's advice sharper.

The image (use it here): “Think of a millstone. First turn, it grinds slowly — but the grain passing through sharpens the stone. By harvest it cuts finer than any mill your competitor owns. They can't buy that sharpness. It's ground in.”

Attach the caveat — every time, unprompted: “To be straight with you: in v1 that loop closes through your team acting on the data — your agronomists and procurement. It's not a model retraining itself. The data compounds in one place; your people act on it.”

3The Close

Close: “Every field your consultants walk this season is either training your system — or training no one.”

If they push — pre-empted answers

They say / askYou say
CFO: "How does this lower customer acquisition cost / improve the economics over time?" Do not claim a CAC curve without numbers. Pivot to what's provable: "The defensible part isn't a CAC slogan — it's that this dataset can't be bought. It needs the distribution network, the field moment, and the transaction in one place. A standalone app or an ERP has at most one of the three."
Procurement / Ops: reacts to "automated" — hears job threat or compliance risk. Never say "automated". Say "accelerates". "A human approves every quote before it's binding — the system removes the 40 minutes of lookup, not the judgement."
"Does the AI learn and improve by itself?" Honest, first: "No — and we won't pretend otherwise. In v1 the data compounds in one place and your team acts on it. No self-retraining." Then the credibility fact below.
"Won't farmers distrust a phone diagnosis?" Reframe the trust anchor: "The consultant is the trust anchor, not the app. This makes a consultant who's already trusted faster and more defensible — 'here's why I recommended this, and the system backs me.'"
Technical buyer: "How do I know the three agents are really one system, not three tools?" The one credibility fact: "A, Flow and Insight all write to and read from the same event log, in the same database. It's shared state, not an integration. One place where all three meet."
SAY / DON'T SAY
✓ Say
  • "Before the farmer walks back to the truck"
  • "Accelerates" the quote
  • "The consultant is the trust anchor"
  • "Data nobody else can assemble — distribution + field + transaction"
  • "Your team acts on the data" (the honest loop)
  • "One event log — shared state"
✗ Don't say
  • "Automated" quotes / orders
  • "The AI learns and improves itself"
  • "Lowers CAC every cycle" (no cohort data)
  • Lead with the vendor loop on a cold call
  • "Self-funding flywheel" as a standalone claim
  • "Replaces your operations team"

The discipline behind this sheet

  • Moat that's real (lead here if pressed): the dataset geometry — distribution network + field moment + transaction together. A competitor can't replicate it in under ~5 years. Plus growing switching cost once Agro-Flow lives in the Salesforce workflow.
  • Slogan to avoid: "lowers CAC each turn" — plausible mechanism, zero cohort evidence. A sharp CFO dismantles it in 90 seconds. Hold it until there are numbers.
  • Sequencing truth: the whole loop stalls at Beat 1 if Agro-Vision's accuracy doesn't earn consultant trust fast. That's why v1 is Agro-Vision first — trust is the gate, not a given.
  • Know the room: identify the economic buyer before you choose how far into the flywheel to go. Operator → stay concrete. Strategic/exec who asks about defensibility → open the gate, caveat attached.
